# Face 2 Face Prospect Questionnaire

This comprehensive questionnaire is designed to uncover the deep frustrations users experience with traditional swipe-based dating apps and to validate the real-world, open-communication philosophy of Face 2 Face. It is divided into two phases: General Public (Phase 1) and In-App Test Users (Phase 2). 

Please select the questions that resonate most with your target demographic.

## Phase 1: General Public Questionnaire

### Section 1: The Illusion of the "Algorithmic Match" & Restrictive Barriers
*Goal: Dismantle the comfort of mutual matching and expose how algorithmic barriers restrict genuine human interaction.*

1. **Yes/No:** Do you feel forced to rely on an "algorithmic match" rather than your own intuition when trying to connect with someone?
2. **True/False:** The requirement to "mutually match" feels more like a restrictive barrier than a safety measure.
3. **Multiple Choice:** When an app dictates who you can talk to based on an AI match or statistical match, it feels:
   - A) Cold and calculated.
   - B) Restrictive and frustrating.
   - C) Like the app is playing moral guide.
   - D) All of the above.
4. **Ranking:** Rank your frustration with paywalled communication (1 = Not frustrated, 5 = Extremely frustrated).
5. **Yes/No:** Would you prefer an environment that restores traditional ways of finding someone by allowing open communication instantly?
6. **True/False:** I have missed out on genuine connections because a restrictive barrier prevented me from sending the first message.
7. **Multiple Choice:** How do you feel about algorithms controlling your social visibility?
   - A) It ruins the organic nature of meeting people.
   - B) It feels like constant manipulation.
   - C) It's acceptable but annoying.
8. **Yes/No:** Do you believe dating apps act as gatekeepers rather than facilitators?
9. **True/False:** Open communication without an arbitrary algorithmic match would lead to more honest interactions.

### Section 2: Psychological Impact & The Addiction Cycle
*Goal: Highlight the negative psychological effects of continuous swiping and the delete/re-download cycle.*

10. **Yes/No:** Have you ever deleted a dating app out of frustration, only to re-download it a few days or weeks later?
11. **True/False:** The endless swiping mechanic feels designed to foster addiction rather than help me find a partner.
12. **Multiple Choice:** How does the continuous cycle of swiping affect your mental health?
    - A) Breeds confusion and exhaustion.
    - B) Creates a false sense of self-worth (e.g., matching with hundreds but connecting with none).
    - C) Destroys self-esteem due to low interaction rates.
    - D) Creates a numbing effect toward real people.
13. **For Women (Yes/No):** Do you feel that possessing hundreds of unanswered matches gives a false sense of validation without yielding real-world results?
14. **For Men (Yes/No):** Does the low algorithmic match count on traditional apps negatively impact your self-esteem?
15. **True/False:** The gamification of dating apps strips away the humanity of the people on the screen.
16. **Multiple Choice:** What is the primary emotion you feel after 15 minutes of continuous swiping?
    - A) Burnout.
    - B) Anxiety.
    - C) Boredom.
    - D) Hopeful.
17. **Yes/No:** Do you feel that traditional apps use your loneliness against you to keep you swiping?
18. **True/False:** The isolation of profile decks makes me feel less connected to my local community.

### Section 3: The Dark Side of Competitors (Scams, Data, Exploitation)
*Goal: Tear down competitor apps (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge) by exposing their failures in privacy and safety.*

19. **Yes/No:** Are you aware that many traditional dating apps retain your personal data for extended periods even after you delete your account?
20. **True/False:** I frequently encounter scammers, bots, or fake profiles on apps like Tinder or Bumble.
21. **Multiple Choice:** How concerned are you that dating apps sell user data (including biometric data from facial verification) to third parties?
    - A) Extremely concerned.
    - B) Somewhat concerned.
    - C) Not concerned.
22. **Yes/No:** Do you trust how major dating corporations handle your private photos?
23. **True/False:** It feels like major dating apps exploit user photos to populate their ecosystems or train AI models without explicit consent.
24. **Multiple Choice:** What is the most egregious failure of current mainstream dating apps?
    - A) Profiting off prolonged singleness.
    - B) Failing to protect users from professional scammers.
    - C) Shady data retention and privacy policies.
    - D) All of the above.
25. **Yes/No:** If an app guaranteed your data was permanently erased upon deletion, would you be more likely to use it?
26. **True/False:** The so-called "safety features" of Bumble and Tinder fail to actually keep bad actors off the platform.
27. **Yes/No:** Do you feel exploited when apps put basic communication features behind expensive paywalls?

### Section 4: Direct Interaction & App Philosophy
*Goal: Validate the Face 2 Face approach of open communication and passing by.*

28. **Yes/No:** If you saw someone interesting on a map, would you prefer the ability to initiate a "direct interaction" rather than hoping for a statistical match?
29. **True/False:** If someone reaches out and I am not interested, I prefer to simply "pass by" them rather than actively rejecting or ignoring them.
30. **Multiple Choice:** Instead of playing matchmaker, an app should:
    - A) Simply show you who is around and get out of the way.
    - B) Carefully curate your feed based on extensive personality algorithms.
    - C) Act as a strict moral guide on how you interact.
31. **Yes/No:** Do you prefer an app that restores traditional rules of engagement (e.g., walking up to someone) in a digital format?
32. **True/False:** Direct messaging someone in your vicinity feels more authentic than swiping right on a static profile from miles away.
33. **Multiple Choice:** How should a platform handle unwanted attention?
    - A) Allow users to quickly "pass by" and remove the person from their map.
    - B) Prevent anyone from messaging without mutual permission first.
34. **Yes/No:** Does waiting for a mutual swipe kill the spontaneity of a real-life encounter?

40. **Multiple Choice:** How should an app handle highly specific or traditional lifestyle tags?
