## 3. WHAT ACQUIRERS LOOK FOR

### Match Group Criteria
1. Niche demographic segmentation (Hinge→serious, BLK→Black, Chispa→Latino)
2. Geographic expansion (Pairs→Japan, Happn→Europe)
3. Technology/IP differentiation (Hyperconnect: 8.6x!)
4. Hyper-growth potential (Hinge: $8M→$691M)
5. Backend synergy (shared safety, AI, payments)

### Key Metrics Driving Valuation
- DAU/MAU 20%+, session frequency
- D1/D30 retention
- Free→Paid conversion 4-7%
- ARPU/ARPPU trends
- 25%+ YoY revenue growth = premium

## 4. IPO BENCHMARKS

### Minimum for Dating App IPO
- Revenue: $180M-$250M+
- Growth: 30-40%+ annually
- EBITDA margin: 15%+
- Rule of 40: Revenue growth + EBITDA > 40%
- Audited financials: 2+ years
- IPO prep time: 18-24 months

### F2F IPO Assessment
- Not viable currently
- Realistic at $200M+ ARR, 30%+ growth — likely 5-7 years minimum
- More realistic: strategic acquisition or PE buyout

## 5. FAILED EXITS

### Clover — Bankruptcy (2023)
- Couldn't compete. Features alone don't create defensibility.

### Coffee Meets Bagel — Declined $30M (2015)
- Turned down Shark Tank offer. Never achieved breakout scale.

### Why Dating Apps Fail
1. Built-in churn (success = user leaves)
2. Unsustainable CAC from high churn
3. Match Group dominance
4. Dating fatigue
5. Monetization difficulty
