🎤 Investor Meeting Prep

The 20 hardest questions investors will ask — with battle-tested answers

❓ "How do you prevent stalking?"

Answer:

"Three layers: 1) Location is fuzzy — we show a 200m radius, not exact coordinates. 2) Users can go invisible instantly. 3) Integrated panic button via Noonlight with average 10-second emergency dispatch. We're building safety features that Tinder still doesn't have after 12 years."

❓ "Why won't Tinder just copy you?"

Answer:

"Tinder makes $1.8B/year from addictive swiping. A live map that facilitates real meetups would cannibalize their own engagement loop. It's the Innovator's Dilemma — their business model depends on you staying single and swiping."

❓ "How do you solve the cold start / empty map?"

Answer:

"City-by-city launch starting with Guadalajara — 350K students, tech-hub DNA. Pre-seed venues on the map before user launch. Scheduled meetup events create guaranteed pins. We only need 50-100 concurrent users per km² to feel alive."

❓ "Why 3 pillars? Isn't that unfocused?"

Answer:

"Three pillars solve the chicken-and-egg problem. A dating-only app needs gender balance Day 1. But Social and Business users create map density regardless of gender. When the map feels alive, dating users stay. WeChat proved the super-app model in China."

❓ "What's your unfair advantage?"

Answer:

"1) Bilingual founder native to Mexico — our launch market. 2) No-algorithm architecture means 10-100x cheaper to serve per user. 3) Three pillars create a network effect flywheel no single-pillar competitor can replicate. 4) POF proved a solo founder can build a $575M dating company."

❓ "What's your path to $100M ARR?"

Answer:

"5M MAU × 6% conversion × $12/mo ARPU = ~$43M ARR from subscriptions alone. Add venue partnerships ($2-5 ARPU from Business pillar) + event ticketing (10% take rate) = $80-120M blended. Bumble hit $1B ARR with 50M users. We need 1/10th of that."
